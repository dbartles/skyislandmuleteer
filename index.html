HOW TO SURVIVE

The Premise

You are a muleteer — a mule driver hauling cargo across the 1850s Arizona and Sonora desert. Accept contracts in towns, load your mules, and deliver goods through hostile territory. Inspired by Cormac McCarthy's Blood Meridian.

Your Character @

You have HP (health), thirst, gold, attack/defense stats, and equipment slots for head, body, feet, and weapon. Thirst drains as you walk — reach 0 and you start losing HP. Find water to survive.

Your Mules m

Mules follow you in a snake-like train. Each mule carries cargo, has its own thirst and HP, and can be spooked by cacti or combat. Spooked mules scatter and must be recovered. Dead mules drop their cargo. Water your mules at cenotes, creeks, and springs or they will die.

Towns & Campfires

Walk to the campfire * at the center of a town to open the town menu. From there you can rest, water mules, buy mules, visit the shop, hit the tavern, view contracts, check inventory, or deliver cargo.

Contracts

Accept contracts at the origin town. You need enough mules to carry the cargo. Deliver to the destination town to collect your pay. Bigger hauls pay more but attract more danger.

Combat

Walk into an enemy to attack. Your weapon and stats determine damage. Enemies drop loot when killed. Beware: enemies also attack your mules.

Survival Tips

O Cenotes, ~ creeks, and o springs restore thirst — walk onto them.

* Cholla cactus deals 2 damage and spooks mules — avoid it!

& Ore veins can be mined for gold, silver, and turquoise (press M).

> Mine entrances lead to underground tunnels with rich ore (press ENTER).

Canyons are ambush territory — Apaches and mountain lions lurk there.

Buy bandages and laudanum before long hauls. Whiskey heals too.
